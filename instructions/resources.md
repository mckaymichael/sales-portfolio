**Shuswap Strong Artist’s Statement**

Over the summer of 2023, my home was nearly burnt down by a vicious forest fire. The forest fire was considered one of the fastest spreading fires in BC history, growing 20 kilometres wide in just 12 hours. The residents of Shuswap Lake and Adams Lake were left fleeing their homes with the fire being less than 100 meters away before they realized it. My dad and my uncle have a business up in the Shuswap that was burned in half. It puts into perspective the selectiveness that a forest fire has. It hops, skips, bounces, dodges, and lands on whatever it feels like. No one knew if the town of Scotch Creek was going to be safe. Luckily, not all of it was burnt down. The fire was savage, and it made some residents turn savage too by using the distraction of the fire as an opportunity to steal from vacant homes and break into facilities such as my father's and my uncle’s. 

Despite the darkness of what happened, an incredible union was formed from the locals who decided to fight back against the raging fire. My uncle fought off the fire before the firefighters and helicopters could help and managed to stop some of the fire in its tracks, saving his and his neighbours' homes. Sparked by these heroic actions that occurred all over the town, the locals of Shuswap created the tag “#ShuswapStrong,” and that became the symbol, thanks to the heroes of our town. 

My photo was shot from my drone, near the end of the fire's rage. The shot is taken from above, looking straight down at the ground. When you look closely, the trees you see are the shadows of the trees still standing. This photo was taken in the morning while the sun was still low and cast these long silhouettes of the burnt-up forest near my dad’s burnt-down business. When I see this photo, I think of what my uncle did to save the homes of his neighbours. I think of the locals everywhere else that were brave enough to fight against this fire. And I think of the strength of the community and how everyone is doing their best to lift each other.

To learn more about the story, you can read it here: https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/british-columbia/wildfires-shuswap-kelowna-august-20-1.6941821

**EcoMeter - https://www.enactusbcit.ca/ecometer | https://eco-meter.ca/ | https://www.linkedin.com/company/ecometer-vancouver/**
*   **Project name:** EcoMeter.
*   **Caption:** "Trust in EcoMeter today. Achieve zero waste tomorrow."
*   **Unique Value Proposition:** EcoMeter serves as both a sustainability consultancy and a public ranking system. It gathers data across food, packaging, and supplier waste to generate a score that acts as both business intelligence—pinpointing operational waste to save money—and sustainability intelligence measuring landfill impact. It turns a sustainability problem into a financial benefit, leveraging the fact that every dollar put into reducing food waste can yield 14 times that in savings.
*   **Creditability/Impact:** The project built a recyclability list in partnership with Metro Vancouver and operates with partners like Dalina Cafe. Based on the performance of similar business models, EcoMeter projects they can save restaurants 2 percentage points off their food and packaging costs within a month. 
*   **Mission statement:** To tackle waste in the food industry by proving that "at EcoMeter we hate waste, and you should too. So, lets avoid it".
*   **Vision Statement:** To help society "achieve zero waste tomorrow" and to build partnerships between restaurants and nonprofits by 2027 to tackle the UN’s Zero Hunger goal.
*   **About the project (in depth):** EcoMeter conducts a two-week waste audit for restaurants by tracking beginning, purchased, and ending inventory. The team installs categorized bins and scales to track spoilage, prep, and plate waste. They also assess how locally a restaurant shops to avoid community waste, and they rate packaging based on local recycling challenges rather than mere manufacturer claims. Restaurants are given a grace period after their first audit to implement sustainable practices and connect with green suppliers before their score is made public through a customizable website widget.
*   **Funding:** EcoMeter is designed to be self-sustaining through two internal revenue streams rather than external funding. They charge a $200 fee for waste audits, which covers necessary materials and their consulting service, and a $1,500 annual fee to suppliers who wish to be prioritized and featured in EcoMeter's recommendation engine.

**Enactus BCIT High School Case Competition - https://www.enactusbcit.ca/new-page-2**
*   **Project name:** Enactus BCIT High School Case Competition.
*   **Caption:** "The Enactus BCIT High School Case Competition empowers aspiring business students with the foundation and experience, but inspires entrepreneurial action in all of us."
*   **Unique Value Proposition:** It bridges the gap between what students learn in school and what employers expect in the workforce by providing hands-on experiential learning. Instead of abstract hypotheticals, high school students work with real, current data and organizational constraints to build crucial soft skills like communication, teamwork, and problem-solving.
*   **Creditability/Impact:** Founded in 2018, the project engaged 54 high school students from Burnaby and New Westminster this year. 18 BCIT student mentors were trained, and 12 Enactus BCIT members contributed nearly 300 volunteer hours to execute the event. The competition is also gaining regional attention, with Camosun College exploring how to launch a similar competition on Vancouver Island.
*   **Mission statement:** To provide hands-on experiential learning that helps students apply their knowledge and build the skills employers need.
*   **Vision Statement:** To empower aspiring business students, inspire entrepreneurial action, and eventually reconnect winning high school teams with industry leadership to refine and implement their competitive recommendations.
*   **About the project (in depth):** The competition is a four-step process across two phases delivered in partnership with the BCIT School of Business + Media. Phase one focuses on mentorship, where BCIT students are trained in business frameworks and paired with high school teams for after-school guidance sessions. Phase two is the competition day, where students have four hours to analyze a case study and present solutions to industry judges. This year, the case study was centered directly on EcoMeter, allowing students to act as consultants for an actively evolving Enactus project.
*   **Funding:** The competition is funded through sponsorships and a participation fee of $150 per team. This year, it earned a total of $5,850 in revenue against costs of about $5,200. *Note: The sources do not detail what the remaining $650 profit is used for.*

**Lumora - https://www.linkedin.com/company/lumorainc/posts/?feedView=all**
*   **Project name:** Lumora.
*   **Caption:** "Transforming unavoidable milk waste into a biodegradable fibre that works with nature rather than against it."
*   **Unique Value Proposition:** Lumora solves two major environmental issues at once: microplastic contamination in agriculture and the dumping of millions of litres of excess milk due to dairy quotas. Using a patented, formaldehyde-free chemical process, Lumora converts waste casein proteins from milk into a 100% biodegradable, compostable, and non-toxic fibre that can replace agricultural plastic while releasing nitrogen to enrich the soil.
*   **Creditability/Impact:** The project recently graduated from the League of Innovators Startup Accelerator. Lumora can produce up to 100kg of fibre for every 5,000 litres of wasted milk. They project that by their second year, they will achieve a monthly profit of over $6,000 while saving over 67,000 litres of milk.
*   **Mission statement:** To break the cycle of plastic contamination by transforming unavoidable milk waste into a natural fibre, creating value for farmers and the communities that depend on local food systems.
*   **Vision Statement:** To stop plastic contamination where it begins by initially replacing agricultural plastics on farms, with the understanding that the long-term possibilities for a truly biodegradable plastic alternative are endless.
*   **About the project (in depth):** Lumora gathers excess milk that farms are legally barred from selling and puts it through a chemical process that extracts casein proteins, leaving water and whey as byproducts. This creates a flexible fibre that can be spun into agricultural twine using existing textile machinery. They are currently finalizing R&D and product testing to ensure proper tensile strength and durability for farm use. The project is poised to take advantage of new legislative policies (like Bill C-59 and the Greening Government Strategy) that make corporate sustainability a compliance requirement rather than a branding choice.
*   **Funding:** Lumora's go-to-market strategy requires an initial $80,000 in capital expenditure. This funding is needed to begin commercial production for a pilot plan in Abbotsford (producing 5 tons of fibre a year) through a combination of in-house manufacturing and licensing, which will allow them to operate at a 58% gross profit. *Note: The sources do not explicitly state if this $80,000 has been raised yet or where it is coming from.*
