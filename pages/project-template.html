Lumóra

Project Abstract

Repurposing hotel waste into sustainable sanitation solutions for communities in need.

Unique Value Proposition

Intercepting landfill-bound hygiene products and redistributing them as life-saving sanitation tools.

Impact Metrics & SDGs

10k+ Bars Distributed SDG 3 SDG 6 SDG 12

The Mission

To eliminate preventative hygiene-related diseases by providing access to repurposed soap and sanitation education.

The Vision

A world where every individual, regardless of their socioeconomic status, has equitable access to basic hygiene necessities, fostering healthier and more resilient communities globally.

About Lumóra

Every single day, thousands of partially used soap bars are discarded by hotels across North America. Simultaneously, vulnerable communities both locally and internationally face severe health crises due to a lack of basic sanitation supplies. Lumóra exists to bridge this critical gap.

Operating out of British Columbia, our student-led team partners with regional hospitality providers to intercept landfill-bound hygiene products. Instead of becoming environmental waste, these commodities are collected, sterilized, and meticulously re-processed into brand-new, pathogen-free soap bars.

But our model goes beyond simple distribution. We couple our sanitation products with comprehensive hygiene education, working directly with NGOs and community leaders to ensure that the tools we provide translate directly into long-term behavioral change and disease prevention. This localized approach guarantees that our intervention is not just a temporary fix, but a sustainable pivot towards better public health.
