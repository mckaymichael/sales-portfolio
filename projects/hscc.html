HS Case Comp

The Problem

There is a massive divide between theoretical high school curriculums and the practical, high-stakes soft skills demanded by the modern workforce.

Project Abstract

"The Enactus BCIT High School Case Competition empowers aspiring business students with the foundation and experience, but inspires entrepreneurial action in all of us."

Unique Value Proposition

Bridging the gap between school curriculum and workforce expectations by providing hands-on experiential learning using real, live organizational data.

The Mission

To provide hands-on experiential learning that helps students apply their knowledge and build the skills employers need.

The Vision

To empower aspiring business students, inspire entrepreneurial action, and eventually reconnect winning high school teams with industry leadership to refine and implement their competitive recommendations.

About HSCC

The High School Case Competition (HSCC) is delivered in partnership with the BCIT School of Business + Media as a comprehensive two-phase operation. Instead of abstract hypotheticals, high school students work with active, current data to build crucial soft skills like communication, teamwork, and problem-solving.

Phase one focuses heavily on mentorship. Currently enrolled BCIT students are formally trained in business frameworks and paired intimately with high school teams for after-school guidance sessions over the course of several weeks.

Phase two culminates in an adrenaline-fueled competition day on the BCIT campus. Here, students have four hours to analyze an extensive, surprise case study and present their viable solutions to an intimidating panel of industry judges. Interestingly, this year's case study was centered directly on our own EcoMeter project—allowing these high schoolers to act as active consultants for a rapidly evolving Enactus operation.
